Project goal

A subscription does not win on the first order. It wins on the fifth. The goal was to remove the friction that quietly leaked revenue at every step of the member lifecycle: reaching the first meal faster, making the weekly menu choice feel easy instead of overwhelming, and making staying easier than leaving.

Journey mapProcess image

Problems

Research with new and churned members surfaced where intent was leaking. Three patterns carried most of the weight.

01

Too many steps to value

New members hit sign-up walls, plan choices and address forms before seeing a single meal. Doubt set in before the product could prove itself.

02

Choice overload

Hundreds of chef-made meals each week is a strength that read as a chore. Members spent more energy choosing than enjoying.

03

Billing distrust

Any ambiguity about what gets charged and when pushed cautious members to cancel preemptively: the most expensive kind of churn.

Solutions

01

Value before commitment

Reordered onboarding so people browse real meals first and commit last. Fewer steps to the first order, and a reason to finish them.

02

A menu that knows you

Rebuilt the weekly menu around taste, time and diet, surfacing the right dozen meals instead of demanding a scroll through hundreds.

03

Self-serve control

Made pause, skip and edit fully self-serve with billing always visible. The easiest way to keep a member is to stop trapping them.

Onboarding

From landing to first order: browse the chefs and the food before any commitment, then a checkout where the next step is always obvious.
